Kampung Fried Rice (Indonesia Taste)

Description

Nasi goreng kampung is actually a common menu in all over the country. Kampus means village, which means it is a non fancy fried rice. Usually they make it without chicken, but with bean sprout and cabbage instead.

Ingredients

  • 1 plate of leftover rice
  • 1 chicken skillet (cut diced)
  • 4 garlics (chopped)
  • 1 shallot brown (chopped)
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1 tsp sambal terasi
  • 1 tbsp sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • salt
  • pepper
  • sugar
  • cooking oil
  • water

Cooking Instructions

  1. Prepare all ingredients
  2. Heat the pan, sautee garlic and shallots until golden brown and fragrant, then put the chicken in.
  3. Add some water, soy sauce, salt and pepper to the chicken
  4. When the chicken is cooked, put the overleft rice in. Then followed by the sambal terasi or balachan. Season with sweet soy sauce and sugar.
  5. Stir everything together untiul the rice all turn color. Lastly, put the beaten egg in and mix all of them until it is cooked.
  6. Serve with prawn crackers and chopped chillies.
